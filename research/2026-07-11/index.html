GLP-1 and muscle research: July 5-11, 2026

Comparative effects of drugs for adults with overweight or obesity: systematic review and network meta-analysis.

BMJ (Clinical research ed.) · 2026-07-08 · Nong K et al.

This large systematic review and network meta-analysis of 262 randomized trials (nearly 100,000 participants) compared 19 obesity drugs on 24 outcomes. At one year, tirzepatide produced the greatest weight loss among established agents. Notably, tirzepatide reduced fat mass the most (by 25.7%) but also reduced lean mass the most (by 8.3%) among the drugs examined. Subcutaneous semaglutide was the only drug associated with reduced all-cause mortality and myocardial infarction risk.

Why it matters: The direct, quantified comparison of lean mass loss across GLP-1 and other obesity drugs is directly actionable information for readers focused on preserving muscle during weight loss.

Nutritional, functional, and psychological considerations for incretin-based therapies in adults-an EASO, EFAD, and ECPO Consensus Statement.

The lancet. Diabetes & endocrinology · 2026-07-08 · Dobbie LJ et al.

This EASO, EFAD, and ECPO consensus statement addresses nutritional, functional, and psychological considerations for adults using incretin-based therapies (GLP-1 receptor agonists and dual GLP-1/GIP agonists). It synthesizes evidence on protein targets during weight loss, dietary quality, and mitigation of gastrointestinal side effects, and discusses pragmatic approaches to support fat-free mass preservation and physical function, including adequate protein intake and progressive resistance exercise. The statement also calls for future longitudinal research on macronutrient requirements, musculoskeletal outcomes, and post-cessation maintenance.

Why it matters: This is a directly relevant expert consensus document covering protein, resistance exercise, and body composition monitoring for people on GLP-1 medications—the core concerns of this readership.

Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 Receptor Agonist-Based Agents and Body Composition: Filling More Gaps.

Obesity (Silver Spring, Md.) · 2026-07-08 · Dubin RL et al.

This updated narrative review analyzed 40 DXA-based reports of body composition changes with GLP-1 receptor agonist-based agents. The authors found a mean fat-free mass loss of approximately 29.1% of total weight lost (SD 19.0%), which they note is in the upper range of what is seen with other non-surgical obesity interventions, where diet-induced weight loss typically results in about 25% fat-free mass loss. The authors highlight that functional measures of strength and performance remain understudied and call for more data, particularly in high-risk populations.

Why it matters: Quantifying how much of GLP-1-driven weight loss comes from lean mass—and how variable that is—is central to understanding the muscle-preservation challenge for this readership.

Special Considerations When Using GLP-1 Receptor Agonists in the Treatment of Obesity and Diabetes Mellitus Type 2 in Older Adults.

Advances in therapy · 2026-07-09 · Pendrey AG et al.

This narrative review highlights special considerations for using GLP-1 receptor agonists (including semaglutide, tirzepatide, and liraglutide) in older adults with obesity and type 2 diabetes. The authors note that sarcopenia is a particularly relevant concern in this age group and that weight loss efforts with GLP-1 RAs require cautious approaches. Evidence in older adults specifically remains limited, and polypharmacy and side effects add complexity to treatment decisions.

Why it matters: Older adults on GLP-1 medications face heightened sarcopenia risk, making this population a key concern for muscle-preservation strategies.

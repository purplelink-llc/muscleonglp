Comparative effects of drugs for adults with overweight or obesity: systematic review and network meta-analysis.

BMJ (Clinical research ed.) · 2026-07-08 · Nong K et al.

This large systematic review and network meta-analysis of 262 randomized controlled trials (99,791 participants) compared 19 weight-loss drugs across 24 outcomes. Among the key body-composition findings, tirzepatide produced the greatest fat-mass reduction (by 25.7%) but also the greatest lean-mass reduction (by 8.3%). Subcutaneous semaglutide was the only drug associated with reduced all-cause mortality and myocardial infarction risk. Gastrointestinal adverse events were most common with naltrexone-bupropion, oral semaglutide, orforglipron, and tirzepatide.

Why it matters: The direct, quantified comparison of lean-mass loss across GLP-1 and other obesity drugs—with tirzepatide showing the largest lean-mass reduction—is highly actionable context for readers trying to preserve muscle on these medications.

Nutritional, functional, and psychological considerations for incretin-based therapies in adults-an EASO, EFAD, and ECPO Consensus Statement.

The lancet. Diabetes & endocrinology · 2026-07-08 · Dobbie LJ et al.

This EASO-EFAD-ECPO Consensus Statement addresses nutritional, functional, and psychological considerations for adults using incretin-based therapies (GLP-1 receptor agonists and dual GLP-1-GIP agonists). It synthesizes evidence on protein targets during weight loss, dietary quality, and mitigation of gastrointestinal side effects, and discusses pragmatic approaches—including adequate protein intake and progressive resistance exercise—to support preservation of fat-free mass and physical function. The statement also calls for future longitudinal research on macronutrient requirements, musculoskeletal outcomes, and post-cessation maintenance.

Why it matters: This consensus statement directly addresses protein intake and resistance exercise for fat-free mass preservation on GLP-1 therapies, making it one of the most directly relevant documents available for this readership.

Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 Receptor Agonist-Based Agents and Body Composition: Filling More Gaps.

Obesity (Silver Spring, Md.) · 2026-07-08 · Dubin RL et al.

This updated narrative review analyzed 40 DXA-based reports to assess changes in body composition with GLP-1 receptor agonist-based agents. The authors found a mean fat-free mass loss of approximately 29.1% (SD 19.0%) of total weight lost, which they note is in the upper range compared with other non-surgical obesity interventions. The review highlights that diet-induced weight loss typically results in roughly 25% of loss coming from fat-free mass, but variability is high. The authors call for more functional strength and performance data and body-composition assessment in high-risk populations.

Why it matters: Quantifying how much of GLP-1-driven weight loss comes from lean mass—and how that compares to other interventions—is central to the concerns of readers trying to protect muscle on these drugs.

Special Considerations When Using GLP-1 Receptor Agonists in the Treatment of Obesity and Diabetes Mellitus Type 2 in Older Adults.

Advances in therapy · 2026-07-09 · Pendrey AG et al.

This narrative review discusses special considerations for using GLP-1 receptor agonists (including semaglutide, tirzepatide, and liraglutide) in older adults with obesity and type 2 diabetes. The authors highlight that sarcopenia is a particularly relevant concern in this age group when pursuing GLP-1 RA-driven weight loss, and that evidence in older adults specifically remains limited. Polypharmacy and side-effect burden are also flagged as important factors. The review concludes that GLP-1 RAs remain a valuable option but require cautious, age-tailored approaches.

Why it matters: Older adults on GLP-1 medications face heightened sarcopenia risk, making this review directly relevant to readers focused on preserving muscle during weight loss.
